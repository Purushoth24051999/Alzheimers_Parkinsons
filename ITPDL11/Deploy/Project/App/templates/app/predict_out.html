Parkinson's Disease

Description: Parkinson's disease is a progressive nervous system disorder affecting movement.

Symptoms: Tremors, slow movement, stiffness, balance difficulty, depression, sleep issues, cognitive decline.

Behavior: Difficulty walking, talking, and completing daily tasks.

Causes: Breakdown of dopamine-producing neurons. Genetics and environment contribute.

Prevention: No cure, but exercise and a healthy diet help slow progression.

Precautions: Early detection, therapy, medications, and support groups help manage symptoms.

Additional Information

Tremors: Usually begin in the hands or fingers.

Bradykinesia: Slowed movement affecting daily tasks.

Muscle Rigidity: Limits movement.

Postural Instability: Balance issues.

Sleep Disorders: Difficulty sleeping, REM disorder.

Mood Changes: Depression, anxiety, hallucinations.
